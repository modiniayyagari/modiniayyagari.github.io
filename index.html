About Me

Hello, I am Modini Ayyagari, pursuing a Doctorate in Computer Engineering at North Carolina State University, guided by Dr. Aydin Aysu at the Hardware Cyberthreat Research (HECTOR) Lab. My primary research interest lies in the realm of hardware security. I am currently in my first year of pursuing a PhD, focusing on understanding the fundamentals of hardware security, physical side-channels, and fault attacks on cryptographic and AI/ML hardware. In my research I am investigating the dual role of physical side channels in hardware security, exploring their potential to be both offensive and defensive strategies for AI/ML semiconductor chips. Prior to beginning my PhD, I accumulated 4.5 years of industry experience as an RTL Design Engineer at Infineon Technologies. During this time, I developed Soft IPs for CPU Sub-Systems, cryptographic accelerators, memory controllers, data transfer engines, and ML accelerators.

During my tenure as an RTL Design Engineer, I led the development of next-generation ARM Cortex-M85 and Cortex-M55 CPU Sub-Systems, enhancing their micro-architecture to support additional external bus-masters with special access permissions and protection schemes. I also developed and maintained hardware accelerator SoftIPs for cryptography, data transfer engines, and programmable IO, achieving a 200x performance gain for AI accelerator SoftIP. My work included performing latency analysis for SoC Memory SoftIP, and identifying and proposing solutions for performance bottlenecks. I managed memory controller IPs, enhancing their reliability features and implementing new memory macros. Additionally, I mentored new graduates and interns, providing technical guidance and actionable feedback.
